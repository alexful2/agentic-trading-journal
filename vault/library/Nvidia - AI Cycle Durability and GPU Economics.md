# Nvidia — AI Cycle Durability and GPU Economics

The core question for Nvidia isn't this quarter's EPS (well understood in advance by the buy side) — it's the **durability of demand** and the **ROI on AI**, which is tied to the useful life of a GPU.

## The bubble / overbuild frame (Perez)

Every comparable episode in market history — railroads, radio, the internet — produced a financial bubble and an overbuild: markets correctly grasp the technology's potential, excitement drives a bubble, the bubble drives an overbuild, the overbuild causes a demand air-pocket and a crash, and the resulting oversupply of the enabling technology ushers in a "golden age." For Nvidia, the EPS-durability question is everything; the market's valuation can be read as pricing in a *coming overbuild* — a bubble in capex, not in Nvidia's own multiple (tech has at times traded at consumer-staples multiples). Build confidence in even high-single-digit forward revenue-growth durability and the valuation should re-rate up.

## Why "this time may be different": watts and wafers

The world is fundamentally short both **watts** (power) and **wafers** (fab capacity), and resolving those shortages may take years. Those shortages can *prevent* the overbuild — hyperscalers would overbuild if they could, but physically cannot. There were no comparable supply constraints gating the precedent technologies. It's hard to get a crash without an overbuild, especially from valuation levels that aren't themselves bubbly. Disciplined, restraint-minded fab operators who pace capacity expansion may single-handedly smooth and elongate the cycle — which is good for the ROI on AI, not just for the stock.

## GPU rental price = the ROI heartbeat

GPU rental prices reflect the economic value of tokens — they're the beating heart of "ROI on AI." You'd normally expect them to drift down as newer, more efficient GPUs arrive. Instead, rental prices for older GPUs have gone *vertical*, and even much-older generations stay fully utilized at firm pricing. That strongly suggests agentic AI (especially agentic coding) is delivering real economic value, and that the **useful life of a GPU is longer than customers' depreciation schedules assume** (6+ years). Longer useful life → stronger residual values → lower financing rates over time.

## GPU vs. ASIC

A general-purpose, programmable GPU plausibly has a *longer* useful life than an ASIC optimized for a single model or application, because the frontier moves fast and specialization shortens useful life. That makes ASICs harder to finance and gives them a higher cost of capital. Inference increasingly needs a *symphony* of chips working together (networking, memory, accelerators), not a single ASIC — another structural point in the GPU ecosystem's favor.

## Refresh discipline (the actual point of the note)

A single-name thesis is only useful if it's re-checked. On a periodic refresh, score each leg as strengthened / intact / weakening against fresh data — e.g.:
- *Watts-and-wafers* → strengthened if hyperscaler capex keeps climbing and builds are explicitly supply-paced.
- *GPU-rental ROI heartbeat* → strengthened if older-GPU rental rates rise and capacity stays sold out.
- *Fab restraint = good* → add a risk caveat: the same restraint that smooths the cycle is also a single point of failure (geopolitics, input shortages).
- *Bubble/overbuild case* → "alive but not confirming" until rental prices soften or capex-pacing breaks.
