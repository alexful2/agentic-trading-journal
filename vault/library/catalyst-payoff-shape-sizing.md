# Catalyst-Payoff-Shape Sizing (the Mirror Principle)

Position sizing before a catalyst should be a function of the catalyst's payoff
shape, not the direction of the expected move.

**Asymmetric upside with a clean floor** (e.g. a name where an existing
contracted revenue stream floors the bear case): cap *adds*. The risk is chasing
the news after it fires, not the catalyst itself. Let the position run into the
event.

**Symmetric or left-skewed payoff with concentration** (e.g. a richly-valued name
into earnings, where a beat that merely decelerates still disappoints elevated
expectations): cap *holds*. The risk is concentration amplifying a miss. Don't
add into the event.

Same principle, opposite direction. This resolves the apparent contradiction
between "let winners run" and "trim before the binary" — the answer depends on
the shape, not the direction.

The discipline: before any pre-catalyst sizing decision, draw the payoff. Where
is the floor? Is the upside genuinely asymmetric, or are you long a coin flip
sized like a sure thing? The shape decides; the direction of your hope does not.
