# AI Infra Thesis Kill-Switches

Three numerical thresholds that would actually break the AI infrastructure cycle — not volatility, not geopolitics, not a single bad print. The point of writing them down is to separate "the secular thesis is intact, this move is noise" from "the thesis is genuinely breaking."

**1. Capex collapse:** Aggregate hyperscaler capex YoY growth <10% for two consecutive guidance cycles. One soft quarter is noise; two consecutive is a demand signal.

**2. GPU pricing breakdown:** Sustained GPU pricing decline >25% over multiple quarters with lead times collapsing to weeks. That signals supply has structurally overshot demand, not just cyclically.

**3. Credit stress:** BBB credit spreads >250bp or HY spreads >600bp. The buildout is now partly debt-financed, so if credit markets seize, capex commitments get deferred regardless of AI demand.

**How to use:** Run these against the tape. If none are tripped, the secular thesis is intact and single-name moves are noise or opportunity. If one trips, reassess position sizing. If two trip simultaneously, treat the thesis as broken.

## Discount-rate path — not a kill-switch, a multiple-compressor

The three switches above all break the *buildout*. There is a fourth path that breaks the *multiple* on names already building, without the buildout itself stalling: a longer rate-cut wait than the curve is currently pricing. Names underwriting terminal-multiple expansion at extreme price-to-sales need rate cuts to clear the implied cost of capital; a pushed-out easing path compresses what multiple can be defended at today's price. This is a sizing constraint, not a sell signal — capex, GPU pricing, and credit spreads are the demand/supply break-points; the discount-rate path is a *what can I pay today* question.
