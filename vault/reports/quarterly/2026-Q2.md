| NBIS | 2026-05-13 | −7.45% | *above ladder* (closest: Blowout + raise) | **15%** | Hold all 4; no trim | unverified |

**Aggregate signal (n=5):**

- **Upside anchors set too low — clean and consistent across all five.**
  Every print resolved at the *top* price tier of its ladder, and three of
  the five (AMZN, IREN, NBIS) landed in the *lowest-probability* upside
  scenario (15%). The mean assigned probability of the tier the price
  actually hit was ~18%. NBIS printed clean above the entire ladder (top
  zone $175–185; stock was $207 at print close, $191.82 at horizon — a
  boundary miss past the top rail). A ladder whose ~15–18% tier catches the
  realized price five times out of five is **systematically under-weighting
  upside / setting the high zone too conservatively.** The price zones
  appear anchored to pre-print spot and went stale the moment the stock
  gapped on the print. This is the most statistically clean finding of the
  quarter — small n, but 5/5 in one direction.
- **Caveat on the metric.** `matched_scenario` keys off the *absolute*
  horizon price vs. fixed ladder zones, while `realized_move_pct` is from
  print close. CORZ "matched" the top zone on absolute price ($24, top of
  $24–28) yet drifted −2.56%; NBIS gapped up then bled −7.45% but stayed
  above every zone. So the miss is specifically in **where the price
  anchors were set**, not necessarily in the directional call. The fix is
  re-anchoring the ladder to the *post-gap* price, or widening the top
  zone, not re-weighting the probabilities alone.
- **Plan execution is unverified across all five.** No `vault/notes/` entry
  records whether the GTCs/trims actually fired. This is a behavioral
  tracking gap, not a system miss — but it means the "did the pre-commit
  plan work?" half of the calibration cannot run. Worth a one-line
  execution note per print going forward.
- **Tooling fix applied this review:** `score_print.py` originally failed
  on AMZN because it scored only the **gate** file, which carries a
  Pre-Commit Plan but no Scenario Ladder (gate files defer the ladder to
  the initial file). The script now falls back to the initial file's
  ladder when the gate has none (emitting a `ladder_fallback` note). AMZN
  re-scored cleanly, taking the sample from n=4 to n=5.

## Key Takeaways

- **One concrete system change is warranted: tighten the `execution-thesis`
  prompt's primary-source discipline.** 6/6 detailed audits caught the same
  tier-laundering / un-extracted-SEC-figure pattern. Add an explicit
  pre-write step requiring every `[A]` row to cite a direct SEC/FDA URL (not
  a mirror) and to extract the specific filed number. This is a prompt fix,
  not a discipline reminder.
- **Pre-earnings ladders are anchoring the top zone too low.** 5 of 5
  prints landed in or above the top tier; 3 of 5 in the 15% tail. Re-anchor
  the ladder to post-gap price, or widen/​re-weight the top zone. (The
  `score_print.py` gate-fallback bug that had dropped AMZN was fixed during
  this review.)
- **The buy-below entry pattern keeps missing deal-driven names (CORZ, BE).**
  Recurred twice this quarter despite [[feedback_starter_now_over_buy_below]].
  The deep-dive verdict logic should treat a WATCH on a post/pending-deal
  name as a flag to size a lottery starter now, not as a reason to wait for
  a dip that doesn't come.
- **Watch the Leopold pressure test for non-discrimination.** Seven "Confirmed"
  weeks running with recycled phrasing. Not wrong, but worth a deliberate
  "what would flip this to Challenged?" prompt so the test can fail.
- **Thin calibration substrate.** Zero closed trades and all first-cycle
  execution-thesis builds mean retraction rates, watch-list hit rates, and
  verdict-vs-outcome are mostly not computable yet. The first real
  calibration quarter is whichever one follows the first 2–3 closed trades.
