## B Class shares — founder voting math

The two B Class shares are held individually by entities controlled by the Roberts brothers:

- **B Class Share #1:** Awassi Capital Holdings 1 Pty Ltd (ACN 629 820 499) as trustee for The Awassi Capital Trust #1 — controlled by **William Roberts**
- **B Class Share #2:** Awassi Capital Holdings 2 Pty Ltd (ACN 629 819 978) as trustee for The Awassi Capital Trust #2 — controlled by **Daniel Roberts**

Each B Class share confers **21.8% of total voting power** (43.5% combined). Mechanics of the voting weighting are spelled out in the constitution (Schedule 1 — terms applicable to B Shares).

**B Class share redemption:** Required to be redeemed on the **earlier** of:
1. The holder ceasing to be a director due to voluntary retirement
2. Transfer in breach of constitution
3. Liquidation or winding-up of IREN
4. **November 17, 2033** (12 years after IPO listing date)

The 2033 sunset is mechanical — even without governance changes, founder voting control terminates at that date.

## Founders' voting power summary — what to remember

1. **Founders own 4.6% economically but control 43.5% of votes** (as of Aug 31, 2025).
2. **Voting control is dependent on** the two B Class shares. Loss of B Class share requires a director to retire voluntarily, breach the constitution, or company winding-up. Otherwise the B Class shares persist until **Nov 17, 2033**.
3. **Even at full conversion of all convertible notes**, founders would not be diluted out of voting control because the B Class shares are not pro-rata diluted — the voting power formula is fixed.
4. **The 2025 DEF 14A's Director Election Amendment proposes a classified board** for Non-Founder Directors only. If approved, board changes (other than founder seats) require multi-year shareholder pressure rather than single-AGM action. Founder Director seats are designated by B Class share holders, not put to a shareholder vote.
5. **AGM held November 19, 2025** — this is the AGM at which the constitutional amendments were voted on. Outcome of those votes is not captured in the proxy itself; check 8-Ks filed Nov-Dec 2025 (or company press releases) for vote results.
