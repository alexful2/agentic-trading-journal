## Retrospective: what the $62 trigger missed

The [[RKLB-2026-04-23]] deep-dive set the entry zone at $62 — confluence of bear P/S band, 200 DMA, recent swing low, analyst low. The level was honest at the time and the stock was $84.60. Today it's ~$125 — **roughly +48% from the deep-dive date, +100% from the trigger I was waiting on**. The trigger was waiting on a Neutron slip or a soft May 7 print; neither happened. May 7 print was clean, the stock broke out, the trigger was never going to fire absent a tape regime change. The "right move" reading the deep-dive in real time would have been a starter at the mid-$80s rather than the all-or-nothing $62 confluence. Lesson — [[extended-with-vs-without-catalyst]] applies to space names too: an aggressive bear-band trigger that requires negative news to fire is a *bet against execution*, not a *price entry*. When the company keeps executing, the trigger keeps moving away. This is the same pattern that almost cost me Bloom Energy at $135–150 — see [[BE - 2026-04-30 Retrospective]].

What I would change: at the deep-dive stage, the right structure was probably a small starter at the then-current level *plus* a larger tranche at $62, not zero-and-wait. The $10,000-portfolio-ticket starter I take on AI infra names is exactly this shape, and there was no good reason space couldn't use it.

## Why RKLB still fits the [[Space Race]] thesis

The library note names "sideline companies SpaceX needs" and "payload launches and satellite industries will be very profitable." RKLB is the cleanest public expression of that:

- **Reusable-launch cost-collapse pattern** — Neutron is the Falcon 9 challenger. Same physics (reusability is known engineering, not breakthrough science), same incumbent-incentive gap (legacy aerospace makes money selling new rockets, not reusing them).
- **Musk-pattern vertical integration** — Space Systems segment (Optical Support, Precision Components, bus production, propulsion, optical links) is the recurring-revenue government-contract layer that lives underneath the launch optionality. This is the analogue of how SpaceX uses Starlink revenue to underwrite Starship development.
- **Backlog as forward indicator** — $1.85B backlog (+73% YoY at the April print) is empirical pre-commitment to Neutron capacity. Customers are pre-paying for a vehicle that hasn't flown. That's a real signal.
- **Picks-and-shovels structural read** — same logic as the AI-infra cohort, different lane. Don't need to call which launch company wins to invest in the launch *industry*; RKLB is the second public option (SpaceX still private, ASTS is a different niche, the rest are sub-scale).

## Why RKLB doesn't currently fit my portfolio

The [[2026-04-19 IREN thesis]] note and the entire AI-infra rotation queue (IREN, NBIS, CRWV, AMZN, HNGE) have ticker-level entry geometry — buy zones, sizing rules, override clauses, invalidations. Space has had **none** of that. The result: every dollar of marginal capital has had a cleaner home in AI infra. That hasn't changed.

The honest read on today: at $125, RKLB is roughly 47% above the [[RKLB-2026-04-23]] base FV ($84.64) and well into bull-band territory. P/S is now ~120× on $602M TTM revenue (vs. 81× at deep-dive date). The thesis hasn't moved that much in 22 days; the price has. **Not an entry. Not even close.**

## What would change that

Three paths to a real entry, in order of likelihood:

1. **A market-wide risk-off that drags RKLB to $80–95** — beta 2.20 plus a 100% YTD run means a 25–35% pullback is mechanically possible on any macro shock. The August–October window historically has elevated risk (rate-cut delay, tariff escalation, geopolitical tail). $85–95 puts the price back near base FV; $70–80 is the 50 DMA region; sub-$70 would be a structural overshoot.
2. **A Neutron slip or static-fire failure** before Q4 2026 maiden flight — the deep-dive flagged this as the central downside catalyst. Stock could re-rate 25–35% on a credible delay (e.g., another Stage 1 manufacturing issue). Whether to *buy* the slip depends on the cause — manufacturing process problem is fixable; aerodynamic / structural problem is not.
3. **A discrete Space Systems contract win at >$500M scale** — the "deal-strike" leg of the [[wait-for-deal-thesis]] applied to space. NSSL Phase 3 lane assignment, a large NRO award, or a defense prime carve-out. Different from #1/#2: this would be *positive* and would justify paying up at the new fair value.

## Watchlist hygiene

The [[RKLB-2026-04-23]] deep-dive `Buy Below $62` trigger is **stale** at $125 — it implies a 50% drawdown which would itself require a thesis-changing event, and at that point the trigger level is irrelevant (it would need re-derivation from whatever broke). Refreshing it during the next deep-dive run, not now. For the interim, the trigger row stays as documented historical context but doesn't drive action.

**Sizing if/when an entry trigger fires:**
- Starter: 1–3% of portfolio (single $10,000 ticket equivalent).
- Funded from: cash, NOT AI-infra rotations — RKLB is a separate thesis lane and shouldn't compete with IREN/CRWV capital. If cash is tight, RKLB stays at zero rather than displacing committed AI-infra positions.
- Invalidation: Neutron slip past Q2 2027, OR Space Systems revenue growth falls below 25% YoY for two consecutive quarters, OR P/S re-rates above 130× without revenue acceleration.

## The honest meta-lesson

Six curator flags is a system signal I ignored. The curator was telling me weekly that a directional theme without ticker-level entry geometry can't be acted on — and the right response wasn't "wait until the price is right," it was "write the thesis so I'm *ready* when the price is right." I didn't, the price ran, and now I'm writing the same thesis at 2× the deep-dive's anchor price. **Build entry geometry before you need it, not after.**
