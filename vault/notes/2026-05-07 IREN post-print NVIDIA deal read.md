**What the NVIDIA deal actually is — three pieces, unequal weight:**

1. **$3.4B / 5-yr AI Cloud contract.** Real binding revenue (~$0.7B ARR ramping early 2027). But it's **35% the size of Microsoft's $1.94B ARR**. Initial deployment is **only 60MW** vs MSFT's 200MW. Calling it a "second Microsoft" is overreach — NVIDIA isn't a hyperscaler. They have internal compute needs (DGX Cloud, demos, R&D, NVIDIA-branded enterprise services) plus strategic interest in propping up the neocloud ecosystem.

2. **The circularity is real.** NVIDIA sold IREN $5.8B of GPUs (Dell purchase agreements). Now NVIDIA is paying IREN $3.4B over 5 years to use a portion of those GPUs back. Net lifetime: NVIDIA still net-receives more from IREN than vice versa. This is exactly the [[ouroboros-economy-circular-financing]] pattern NVIDIA ran with CRWV / Nebius / Lambda / OpenAI: take equity + give offtake to customers to capture the cloud markup on top of the GPU sale. Don't mistake this for organic third-party demand.

3. **30M-share warrant @ $70 strike, 5-yr.** This is the most interesting piece but it's **an option, not committed capital**. NVIDIA put $0 cash on the table today. At $56.85 close they're out-of-money; wouldn't exercise unless stock is well above $70 (probably $90+). "Regulatory-conditioned" = CFIUS/antitrust review. The implicit $70 floor is a *signal*, not a guarantee. The "$2.1B equity injection" is conditional, not banked.

4. **The 5GW DSX "partnership"** is mostly marketing language. "Intend to support" deployment of up to 5GW = non-binding intent. Real value: customer-acquisition halo from being NVIDIA-preferred. Empty if no further NVIDIA contracts materialize.

- **Thesis is more derisked than a week ago** (Sweetwater energized, second hyperscaler-class anchor signed, software stack acquired via Mirantis, EU footprint via Nostrum, $3.1B ARR contracted vs. effectively $0 outside MSFT 7 months ago).
