First dedicated IREN thesis note. Bought ~$10,000 on April 16 from the deep-dive ADD verdict. Need to reconcile in Journalit at some point.

**Why I own it:**

- Mining-to-AI conversion is the cleanest expression of the [[wait-for-deal-thesis]] I've found. IREN owns 4.5 GW of secured grid power, which takes 5–7 years to replicate. That's a structurally scarce asset in an AI buildout where power is the binding constraint.
- Microsoft deal is the thesis executing in real time — $9.7B contract, $1.9B prepaid, 140K GPUs, path to $3.4B ARR by end-2026. Not a hope-bet; the revenue is contracted.
- Fits the long-term AI-capex thesis — picks-and-shovels layer, directly monetizing the compute scale-up.

**The dilution fear I've been wrestling with:**

- The $6B ATM and JPM's $24 Underweight are real concerns. Genuinely changes the per-share math if they issue meaningful equity at $48.
- But zooming out: **every AI infra company I've looked at has had something seemingly-major wrong with it at initial glance.** CRWV had debt and no profitability — struck an Anthropic deal and ripped 50%. NVDA had supply constraints. SMCI had accounting issues. Every single one was a great buy anyway because the macro catalyst (compute demand → AGI race) overwhelmed the specific concern.
- The CRWV lesson specifically: I was scared off by analyst reports on debt/profitability and nearly missed the move. I don't want to repeat that pattern with IREN.
- If the long-term AI-capex thesis is right — and the evidence keeps compounding (Claude Mythos coming, OpenAI Spud coming, Agentic AI, recursive self-improvement loops, US-Anthropic relationship rebuilding likely for Mythos) — then short-term dilution math is noise against the secular wave.
- **Important distinction I had wrong initially:** Sweetwater 1 energization itself is *not* a dilution event. That capex is already covered by Microsoft prepayments + the $3.6B term loan. The $6B ATM is for everything *beyond* Microsoft — Australia, Childress uncontracted, future hyperscaler deals. Sweetwater energization is pure execution-derisking, not a new funding event.

**Macro context I keep forgetting:**

- Almost every AI infra stock on my watchlist has the same chart right now: dipped on the March 30 US-Iran tensions, shot up 20–50% over the following 2 weeks. IREN's current price isn't IREN-specific strength — it's market-wide beta.
- So if the broader market dumps again (very possible, strait of hormuz, etc.), IREN dumps with it. That's fine. That's opportunity. Selling on macro panic is what made me miss Bloom Energy.
- Mythos and similar catalysts aren't priced in. Neither AMZN nor CRWV moved on the Mythos announcement — the concept of rapid AGI progression is just too unbelievable to most of the market. I think that gap is where the alpha sits.
