**Retrospective on:** [[BE - 2026-04-14 Speculation]]

---

On April 14 BE was at ~$220 post-Oracle and I wrote "foolish to buy now, wait for a dip." Two weeks later BE is near $300. The pullback I was certain had to come never came.

**The misclassification:** I read "extended price + recent rip" as FOMO. The actual signal was "thesis just got dramatically stronger via Oracle and the market is repricing." From a chart they look identical. The difference is whether the underlying business changed, and Oracle changed it.

**Rule for future agents (and me):** when a name is up sharply because a *thesis-confirming* catalyst just dropped, "don't chase FOMO" is the wrong frame. The right frame is "the price now reflects the new thesis — is the new thesis worth owning at the new price?" Not the same question. If yes, paying up is rational, not FOMO.

The general FOMO blind spot still stands — most extended runs are noise. The rule isn't "extended = don't chase," it's "extended **without a fresh thesis-confirming catalyst** = don't chase." The BE-post-Oracle arc is the counter-case.

**CLAUDE.md note:** the "Known blind spots" line still cites "FOMO after a missed move (BE post-Oracle)" as a blind spot. That framing is now contradicted by this retrospective — should be revised when convenient so future agents don't keep pulling BE specifically into the FOMO frame.
