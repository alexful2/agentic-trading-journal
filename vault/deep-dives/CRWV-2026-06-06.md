### Bull

That $99.4B is contracted, take-or-pay, with Meta ($21B) and Anthropic on 15-year terms; the June 2 Chicago deal added $2.2B fully leased. NVIDIA doesn't overpay for its own demand signal. The mechanism the market is missing: 2026 capacity is "largely sold out with prices increasing across the board," so compounding off +111.6% YoY isn't speculative — it's contracted and re-pricing upward. ### First-Principles
CoreWeave is a specialized landlord: it buys NVIDIA GPUs, wraps them in power, cooling, and orchestration tuned for >95% utilization, and rents clusters to entities that need compute faster than they can self-build (6–9 months vs 12–24), with NVIDIA's preferential allocation as the edge. That's a real service at 69% gross margin. The structural question is durability: the asset depreciates fast, the financing is enormous, and demand concentrates in AI labs whose own revenue isn't yet self-sustaining — the circular-financing risk; the $99B backlog is only as good as its counterparties' funding. Backlog diversification away from non-IG AI-natives (<30% now) toward financials and life sciences directly attacks that weakness and is the single most important positive. Ground-up: replacement cost on 3.5 GW contracted power ≈ $59B haircut-adjusted, and $54.8B sits just below it. The business earns its price. Structural fair value: **$50–75B, contingent on counterparty quality holding.**

**Council synthesis:** Bull and First-Principles agree the asset is real and roughly fairly priced ($50–75B vs $54.8B); the Contrarian's financing/ramp critique is the binding risk, not a thesis-killer *yet*. All three point at the same fulcrum — the converts and the H2 ramp. That's a HOLD with the fulcrum events written down as tripwires, not an add into the uncertainty.
